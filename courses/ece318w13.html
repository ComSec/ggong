Analog and Digital Communications

Prof. Guang Gong, http://comsec.uwaterloo.ca/~ggong/

Course Outline

This course introduces principle of communication systems. It covers spectral density of deterministic and random analog signals. Thermal noise and the white noise model. Amplitude and angle modulation, generation and detection schemes, effects of noise. Techniques for handling digital signals including sampling and reconstruction, quantization, waveform coding, and time-division multiplexing. Overview of digital communications.

Main Topics

  1. Signals and spectra identities: Review of Fourier series and transform; energy/power-type signals, spectral density; thermal noise, band-limited white Gaussian noise.
  2. Amplitude modulation (AM): Double Sideband Large Carrier (DSB-LC), Double Sideband Suppressed Carrier (DSB-SC), Single-Sideband (SSB), Vesigial Sideband (VSB, modulation and demodulation techniques.
  3. Angle Modulation: Phase Modulation (PM), Frequency Modulation (FM), Bessel functions and FM spectral analysis, Narrowband FM and Wideband FM, Frequency Division Multiplexing (FDM).
  4. Noise in AM/FM Systems: Narrow band representation of white Gaussian noise; signal-to-noise ratio in AM/FM; threshold effect in FM radio.
  5. Analog-to-Digital Conversion: Sampling theorem, practical reconstruction, Time Division Multiplexing (TDM); quantization; waveform coding techniques, Pulse Code Modulation (PCM), Differential Pulse Code Modulation (DPCM), Delta Modulation (DM).
  6. Overview of digital modulation: Pulse Amplitude Modulation (PAM); orthogonal signal representation; Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK), Phase Shift Keying (PSK) and Frequency Shift Keying (FSK); optimal receiver, probability of error.
